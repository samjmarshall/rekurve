## Obvious Gaps & Opportunities

### 🚩 Red Flags for Lead Capture

1. **Minimal Contact Form Fields**
   - Only asks for First Name + Email
   - No way to capture: Project type, Budget, Timeline, Phone number
   - Requires manual follow-up to get qualifying information
   - High friction for serious buyers who want quick quotes

2. **No Business Hours or Response Expectations**
   - Potential customers don't know when to expect a reply
   - After-hours inquiries likely go into a black hole
   - No emergency contact option (unusual for construction)

3. **No Self-Service Quote/Estimate Tool**
   - High-value projects ($500K-$2M+ builds) typically have long sales cycles
   - No way to get ballpark pricing without human interaction
   - Qualifying conversations likely happen one-by-one

4. **Phone-Primary System in 2026**
   - 1300 number suggests high call volume
   - No indication of what happens to calls during site visits, after hours, or when busy
   - Gen Z/Millennial buyers (their future market) prefer text/async communication

5. **No Google Reviews Visible**
   - Could not find Google Business Profile with reviews in search
   - May not be optimizing local SEO or trust signals
   - Potential leads likely checking reviews on Google Maps

### 💡 Potential Pain Points to Explore

Based on business model + gaps above, hypothesize these pain points exist:

**Lead Response:**
- Site visits/on-site work means missed calls → voicemail → delayed responses
- Instagram DMs likely high volume (99K followers) but hard to manage
- After-hours inquiries (evenings/weekends when buyers research homes)
- No 24/7 first response capability

**Qualification:**
- Every inquiry requires manual back-and-forth to understand: Budget, Timeline, Location, Project type, Land status
- High-net-worth clients expect fast, professional responses
- Low-quality leads (tire-kickers) consume same time as serious buyers

**Quoting/Follow-up:**
- Custom builds = complex quoting (not formulaic)
- Likely requires: Site assessment, design brief, cost estimation
- Long sales cycle (months) = easy for leads to go cold without follow-up system
- Multiple team members involved (design + construction) = handoff complexity

**Capacity:**
- 11-50 employees = lean team for luxury builds
- Admin/sales likely shared responsibility (founder? office manager?)
- Growth constraint if lead handling is manual

## Conversation Strategy

### Opening Context (Warm from DM)

Already established:
- ✅ Wife follows them (social proof)
- ✅ Working on similar problem for Creation Homes (volume builder)
- ✅ V2 AI credentials (removes "who is this person?" barrier)
- ✅ They said "keen to hear more" + "team doing everything old-fashioned way"

**They've already told us:**
- Pain exists: "team still doing everything the old-fashioned way"
- Strategic intent: "need to implement AI into everything"
- Urgency: "not taking advantage of this New era"

### Discovery Focus Areas

**Part 1: Understanding Their Business (10-15 min)**

Priority questions:
1. "Your Instagram presence is huge (99K followers) - how many inquiries does that generate per week?"
2. "When someone reaches out via DM, website form, or phone, what's the process from first contact to project proposal?"
3. "Who handles incoming inquiries? Design team, project managers, or dedicated sales/admin?"
4. "Your projects are custom luxury builds - what information do you need before you can even ballpark a quote?"

**Part 2: Exploring Pain Points (15-20 min)**

Priority questions:
5. "You mentioned the team's doing things 'old-fashioned' - what's the most painful part of the current process?"
6. "How quickly can you respond to a new inquiry during business hours? What about evenings/weekends?"
7. "When you're on-site or in design reviews, what happens to incoming leads?"
8. "Have you lost projects because competitors responded faster or had better follow-up?"
9. "Walk me through your quoting process - how long from initial inquiry to delivering a proposal?"
10. "Do most inquiries come with all the info you need (budget, timeline, land status, design brief) or is there back-and-forth?"

**Part 3: Quantifying (5-10 min)**

11. "Roughly how many inquiries per week/month across all channels (Instagram, website, phone, email)?"
12. "What's your typical project value for a custom duplex or luxury home?"
13. "What percentage of inquiries turn into actual projects?"
14. "If you had to guess, how many qualified leads slip through the cracks each month due to response delays or lack of follow-up?"
15. "How many hours per week does the team spend on initial qualification, quoting, and follow-up?"

**Part 4: AI Agent Fit (10 min)**

16. "If every Instagram DM and website inquiry got an intelligent response within 5 minutes - even at 11pm on a Saturday - asking the right qualifying questions, what would that be worth?"
17. "What if a system could collect: Budget range, timeline, land status, design preferences, and contact details before the first human conversation?"
18. "For standard duplex builds, could you define pricing bands or at least ballpark ranges that could be shared automatically?"
19. "Would it help if qualified leads were automatically scheduled into your calendar for design consultations?"
20. "What would you need to see to believe AI could handle luxury home inquiries without feeling 'robotic' or cheap?"

**Part 5: Pilot Fit (5 min)**

21. "We're looking for pilot partners in the building sector who can test this and share results publicly - case study, testimonial, before/after metrics. Would Futureflip be open to that?"
22. "What would success look like for you in 90 days? More qualified leads booked? Faster response times? Less admin overhead?"

## Questions Ready (Not Generic)

### Context-Specific Questions (Use Their Language)

1. **"You posted about finding an AI champion - what sparked that? What problem were you trying to solve?"**
   - Gets to the heart of their pain point in their words

2. **"Your work is featured in Better Homes & Gardens, The Local Project - does that generate inbound leads or is it mostly brand building?"**
   - Understand lead generation beyond Instagram

3. **"When a lead comes in via Instagram DM at 8pm on a Sunday, what's the current experience for them and your team?"**
   - Highlights after-hours gap + emotional impact

4. **"Your contact form only asks for name and email - is that intentional to keep it low-friction, or do you then spend time going back-and-forth to get project details?"**
   - Uncovers qualification pain without criticizing

5. **"For a typical duplex build in Sutherland Shire, what's the range someone should expect to invest? Is that something you could share upfront or does it require full consultation?"**
   - Tests if pricing is standardizable

6. **"You've been in business since 2008 and won awards - have you noticed clients expect faster response times now than 5-10 years ago?"**
   - Validates speed-to-lead thesis without pushing it

7. **"I read you can complete builds in 12-16 weeks, which is incredibly fast. How did you systematize that? Have you applied the same thinking to your sales/inquiry process?"**
   - Positions AI as extension of their existing systems thinking
   - Validates they already value efficiency

8. **"With ~20 projects a year, are you capacity-constrained by build capacity or by qualified leads coming in the door?"**
   - Critical question: Are they supply-limited or demand-limited?
   - If demand-limited: AI agent is perfect solution
   - If supply-limited: May not need more leads (yet)

9. **"If a qualified lead is ready to move forward, what's the bottleneck to getting them started - your availability, design capacity, quoting process?"**
   - Identifies capacity constraints

10. **"At ~20 projects per year, if you converted just 2-3 more qualified leads annually without adding staff, what would that mean for the business?"**
    - Helps them quantify ROI ($520K-$780K more revenue, or $50K-$150K more margin)
    - Makes the case that better lead handling = material business impact

11. **"You mentioned the team is doing things 'old-fashioned' - what's the first thing you'd want to automate or improve?"**
    - Let them tell you the priority
