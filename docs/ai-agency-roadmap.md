# AI Automation Agency: Detailed Roadmaps & Task Lists

## Phase 0: Pre-Launch Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1: Legal & Business Infrastructure
**Priority: CRITICAL - Must complete before client acquisition**

- [ ] **Register ABN** 
  - Visit abr.gov.au
  - Complete online application (15 mins)
  - Receive ABN confirmation (instant)
  - Save ABN certificate to business folder

- [ ] **Register Business Name**
  - Visit ASIC Connect
  - Search available names (3-5 options prepared)
  - Register name ($37 for 1 year, $89 for 3 years)
  - Recommended format: "[YourName] Automation Solutions" or "AI Sales Systems"

- [ ] **Open Business Bank Account**
  - Research options: Commonwealth, NAB, Westpac business accounts
  - Prepare documents: ID, proof of address, ABN certificate
  - Book appointment or apply online
  - Set up separate account for taxes (30% of income)

- [ ] **Research Insurance Providers**
  - Get 3 quotes for Professional Indemnity ($2M coverage)
  - Get 3 quotes for Public Liability ($10M coverage)
  - Compare: BizCover, Aon, Guild Insurance
  - Budget: $1,700-3,500 annually total

- [ ] **Legal Templates Research**
  - Find solicitor for contract templates OR
  - Purchase templates from LegalVision ($300-500)
  - Required: MSA (Master Services Agreement), SOW template, NDA
  - Save to Google Drive business folder

## Phase 1: Month 1 - Launch & Initial Outreach

### Week 1-2: Network Activation
**Goal: 50 conversations, 10 discovery calls, 2-3 pilot clients**

#### Monday-Wednesday: Warm Outreach Preparation
- [ ] **Create Warm Contact List (100 people)**
  - Export LinkedIn connections to spreadsheet
  - Categorize by:
    - **Tier 1 - Potential clients**: People in target industries/roles (20-30)
    - **Tier 2 - Strong referrers**: People who know your target market well (30-40)
    - **Tier 3 - Weak referrers**: General network (40-50)
  - Add columns: Name, relationship, company, last contact, approach angle

- [ ] **Craft Personalized Messages**
  - **Template A - Potential Clients**:
    ```
    Hey [Name], 
    
    I've launched something I think you'd find interesting. I'm helping Brisbane consulting firms recover 20+ hours weekly through sales automation - seeing 50% time savings in early pilots.
    
    Given [Company]'s growth to [X people], I imagine you're feeling the CRM complexity. Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to discuss what's working for similar firms?
    
    No pitch, just curious to hear what you're seeing on your end.
    ```
  
  - **Template B - Referrers**:
    ```
    Hey [Name],
    
    Hope you're doing well! I've started a specialized service helping professional services firms automate their sales processes - working with [insert any early pilot clients] to recover 15-20 hours weekly.
    
    Based on our past work together, I thought you might know 2-3 people facing these challenges. Anyone come to mind who's struggling with CRM chaos or manual follow-up?
    
    Happy to buy you coffee and tell you more about it.
    ```

- [ ] **Send Wave 1: Tier 1 (Potential Clients)**
  - Days 1-3: 10 messages per day = 30 total
  - Personalize EVERY message (reference recent post, company news, mutual connection)
  - Track responses in CRM
  - Goal: 30-40% response rate (9-12 responses)

#### Thursday-Friday: Response Management & Discovery Calls
- [ ] **Respond to All Replies Within 2 Hours**
  - If interested: Send Calendly link immediately
  - If soft no: "No worries! Mind if I ask - what's your current biggest challenge with [sales process]?"
  - If referral: "That's really helpful. Could you intro me via email? Happy to draft a note you can forward."

- [ ] **Prepare Discovery Call Script**
  - Create Google Doc with:
    - Pre-call research checklist (LinkedIn, company website, recent news)
    - Opening questions (5 mins: role, team size, current process)
    - Deep-dive questions (15 mins: pain points, tools, budget, decision process)
    - Next steps (5 mins: "Based on what you've shared, I think [package] would be perfect. I'll send over a proposal by [date].")
  - Practice script 3 times with friend/partner

- [ ] **Conduct First Discovery Calls**
  - Target: 3-5 calls this week
  - Take detailed notes in CRM
  - End each call with: "I'll send you a proposal by [specific day]. Any questions before I put that together?"
  - Send thank you message within 1 hour

### Week 3-4: LinkedIn Cold Outbound + Proposals
**Goal: 100+ new connections, 5 discovery calls, 2-3 pilot clients signed**

#### Daily LinkedIn Routine (2 hours/day - NON-NEGOTIABLE)
- [ ] **Morning Routine (60 mins: 7-8am)**
  - **Connection Requests (20 mins)**: Send 25-50 requests
    - Use Sales Navigator saved search
    - Personalize each request:
      ```
      Hi [Name], I help Brisbane consulting firms recover 20+ hours weekly through sales automation. Noticed you recently [specific detail from profile] - would love to connect and share what's working for similar firms.
      ```
  - **Engagement (20 mins)**: Comment on 10-15 posts
    - Find posts from target ICP using Sales Navigator feed
    - Leave thoughtful comments (2-3 sentences, add value)
    - Don't pitch in comments - build visibility
  - **Content Posting (20 mins)**: Create/schedule 1 post
    - Monday: Personal story/insight
    - Wednesday: Case study/client win (after first clients)
    - Friday: Technical tip/tutorial

- [ ] **Evening Routine (60 mins: 6-7pm)**
  - **Message New Connections (30 mins)**
    - For connections accepted that day, send Message 1:
      ```
      Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Quick question - are you currently using any automation for your sales follow-up process? 
      
      I've been working with [similar firm] to solve [specific problem based on their profile] and seeing some compelling results.
      ```
  - **Follow-up Sequences (30 mins)**
    - Message 2 (3 days after Message 1):
      ```
      [Name], I actually created a quick assessment that identifies the 5 highest-ROI automation opportunities for [their industry] firms. 
      
      Would you be interested in me running it for [Company]? Takes about 15 minutes, completely free, no strings attached.
      ```
    - Message 3 (4 days after Message 2): Share case study
    - Message 4 (7 days after Message 3): Break-up message

#### Proposal Creation & Sending
- [ ] **Create Proposal Template (Monday Week 3)**
  - Use Google Docs or Better Proposals
  - Structure (12-15 pages):
    - **Cover page**: Their logo + your logo
    - **Executive summary**: Their problem in 1 paragraph
    - **Current state analysis**: What you learned in discovery
    - **Proposed solution**: Which package + why
    - **Detailed scope**: Week by week breakdown
    - **Timeline**: Visual Gantt chart
    - **Investment**: Pricing + payment terms (50% upfront, 50% on completion)
    - **Guarantee**: Your specific guarantee language
    - **Case studies**: 1-2 examples (use other industries initially)
    - **Next steps**: How to get started
    - **Signature page**: E-signature field

- [ ] **Send Proposals Within 24 Hours of Discovery Call**
  - Customize template for each prospect
  - Send via email with video message:
    - Record 2-3 min Loom video
    - Walk through proposal highlights
    - Reiterate excitement to work together
    - "I'll follow up on [specific date] but feel free to reach out sooner with questions"

- [ ] **Follow-up Cadence**
  - Day 0: Send proposal + video
  - Day 2: "Just checking you received the proposal. Any initial questions?"
  - Day 5: "Have you had a chance to review? Happy to jump on a quick call to discuss."
  - Day 7: Phone call or video message: "Would love to move forward with you. What questions can I answer?"
  - Day 10: "Should I take this off your plate? I'm happy to circle back when timing is better."

#### Week 4: Close First Pilot Clients
- [ ] **Negotiation & Closing**
  - Be prepared for objections:
    - "Price is too high": "Compared to the $50K you're losing annually to manual processes?"
    - "Need to think about it": "What specifically do you need to think about? Budget, timing, or scope?"
    - "Want to see results first": "That's exactly why we offer the guarantee - you only pay if we deliver results"
  - Use "puppy dog close": "How about this - let's do the audit first (no cost). If you don't see value, no obligation. Fair?"

- [ ] **Sign 2-3 Pilot Clients**
  - Get signed contracts
  - Collect 50% deposit (via bank transfer or Stripe)
  - Send welcome email with:
    - Project kickoff date
    - What you need from them (access to tools, calendar time)
    - What they can expect
  - Schedule kickoff call for following week

### Month 1 KPIs to Track
- [ ] Create KPI dashboard (Google Sheets):
  - Outreach sent: Target 100+ (50 warm, 50+ cold)
  - Response rate: Target 15-20%
  - Discovery calls: Target 10
  - Proposals sent: Target 5
  - Pilot clients signed: Target 2-3
  - Revenue: Target $8,000-15,000

## Phase 2: Month 2 - Delivery Excellence

### Week 1: Project Kickoff & Setup
**Priority: Deliver exceptional results to first clients**

#### For Each Client Project:
- [ ] **Pre-Kickoff Preparation (2 days before)**
  - Research client thoroughly:
    - Review their website, LinkedIn, recent news
    - Audit their current CRM setup (if accessible)
    - Review discovery call notes
  - Create project folder structure:
    ```
    /[Client Name]/
      /01-Discovery/
      /02-Current State/
      /03-Design/
      /04-Implementation/
      /05-Documentation/
      /06-Training/
      /07-Handoff/
    ```
  - Prepare kickoff agenda:
    - Introductions (5 mins)
    - Project goals recap (10 mins)
    - Timeline walkthrough (10 mins)
    - Access & permissions needed (10 mins)
    - Communication plan (5 mins)
    - Q&A (10 mins)

- [ ] **Kickoff Call (60 mins)**
  - Record call (with permission) for documentation
  - Get access to:
    - CRM platform (admin access)
    - Email system
    - Any other tools in tech stack
  - Identify key stakeholders
  - Schedule weekly check-ins (same day/time each week)
  - Set communication preferences (Slack, email, phone)

- [ ] **Week 1: Sales Process Audit**
  - Map current sales process:
    - Create flowchart in Miro/Lucidchart
    - Identify every step from lead capture to close
    - Note time spent on each step
    - Mark pain points and bottlenecks
  - Analyze CRM data:
    - Lead volume and sources
    - Conversion rates by stage
    - Average time in each stage
    - Data quality issues
  - Interview 3-5 sales team members (30 mins each):
    - What takes the most time?
    - What's most frustrating?
    - What do they wish was automated?
  - Create audit report (15-20 pages):
    - Current state summary
    - Key findings (top 5 issues)
    - Quantified impact (hours wasted, opportunities lost)
    - Recommended automations (prioritized)
  - Present findings to stakeholders (60 min meeting)

### Week 2-3: Implementation
- [ ] **CRM Setup & Optimization**
  - Clean existing data:
    - Deduplicate contacts/companies
    - Standardize field formats
    - Archive old/invalid records
  - Restructure pipeline stages:
    - Align with actual sales process
    - Set probability percentages
    - Define entry/exit criteria
  - Create custom fields as needed:
    - Lead score
    - Last contact date
    - Next follow-up action
  - Set up automation rules:
    - Auto-assign leads
    - Task creation triggers
    - Pipeline stage progression
  - Build lead scoring system:
    - Demographic scoring (company size, industry, role)
    - Behavioral scoring (email opens, link clicks, meetings)
    - Configure point thresholds

- [ ] **Email Automation Sequences**
  - Design 3-5 sequences:
    - **New Lead Nurture** (5 emails over 14 days)
    - **Cold Outbound** (4 emails over 10 days)
    - **Meeting Follow-up** (3 emails over 7 days)
    - **Proposal Follow-up** (4 emails over 14 days)
    - **Won/Lost Survey** (1 email)
  - Write copy for each email:
    - Personalization tokens
    - Clear CTA in each
    - Mobile-optimized
  - Set up in n8n or client's email tool
  - Configure triggers and conditions
  - Add unsubscribe handling

- [ ] **Testing Phase**
  - Create test contacts in CRM
  - Run through each automation
  - Verify emails send correctly
  - Check timing and triggers
  - Fix any bugs or issues
  - Document any edge cases

### Week 4: Training & Documentation
- [ ] **Create Documentation**
  - **User Guide** (20-30 pages):
    - Overview of new system
    - How each automation works
    - How to trigger sequences
    - How to troubleshoot common issues
    - Screenshots and GIFs for each process
  - **Admin Guide** (10-15 pages):
    - How to modify automations
    - How to add new sequences
    - How to adjust lead scoring
    - System architecture diagram
  - **FAQ Document** (5-10 pages):
    - Top 20 questions you anticipate
  - **Video Tutorials**:
    - Record 5-10 short videos (3-5 mins each)
    - Screen recordings with voiceover
    - Upload to private YouTube/Vimeo

- [ ] **Team Training Sessions**
  - **Session 1: Overview & Basic Usage** (2 hours)
    - Present new system
    - Walk through common workflows
    - Hands-on practice
  - **Session 2: Advanced Features & Admin** (2 hours)
    - Train admins on backend
    - Show how to modify sequences
    - Q&A and troubleshooting
  - Record both sessions for future reference

- [ ] **Handoff & Go-Live**
  - Final walkthrough with stakeholders
  - Flip switch on automations (start with small subset)
  - Monitor closely for first 48 hours
  - Daily check-ins for first week
  - Weekly check-ins for 30-day support period

### Concurrent: Continue Marketing (20% time)
- [ ] **LinkedIn Activity** (30 mins/day)
  - Continue connection requests: 25/day
  - Post 5× weekly about project learnings:
    - "Week 3 of my first sales automation project: Here's what I learned..."
    - Behind-the-scenes content builds authority

- [ ] **Email Newsletter Launch**
  - Start weekly newsletter: "Sales Automation Insights"
  - Send to growing email list (from lead magnet)
  - Content: 1 tip, 1 case study update, 1 tool recommendation
  - 300-500 words, takes 45 mins to write

- [ ] **Continue Discovery Calls**
  - Target: 2-3 new discovery calls per week
  - Goal: Build pipeline for next month

### Month 2 KPIs to Track
- [ ] Update KPI dashboard:
  - Active client projects: Target 2-4
  - Discovery calls: Target 8-10
  - New clients signed: Target 1-2
  - Revenue: Target $12,000-20,000
  - Client satisfaction (weekly check-ins): Target 4.5+/5
  - Project delivery on time: Target 100%

## Phase 3: Month 3 - Case Studies & Referrals

### Week 1-2: Case Study Development
**Priority: CRITICAL - These become your #1 sales asset**

#### For Each Completed Client Project:
- [ ] **Gather Results Data**
  - Request data from client:
    - Before/after metrics (time saved, conversion rates, pipeline value)
    - CRM reports showing improvement
    - Testimonial quotes from team
  - Calculate ROI:
    - Time saved (hours × hourly rate)
    - Revenue impact (new deals closed)
    - Cost savings (efficiency gained)
  - Take before/after screenshots
  - Record metrics in spreadsheet

- [ ] **Interview Client for Case Study (30-45 mins)**
  - Questions to ask:
    - "What was the main problem we solved for you?"
    - "What were you most skeptical about before starting?"
    - "What surprised you most about the results?"
    - "What specific results have you seen?" (get them to quantify)
    - "How has this changed your team's daily work?"
    - "What would you tell someone considering this?"
  - Record interview (with permission)
  - Transcribe for quotes

- [ ] **Record Video Testimonial (5-10 mins)**
  - Schedule 15-min Zoom call
  - Questions for video (3-4 max):
    - "What was your biggest challenge before working with us?"
    - "What specific results have you seen?"
    - "What would you tell someone on the fence about sales automation?"
  - Edit in Descript or Kapwing
  - Add captions and branding

- [ ] **Write Long-Form Case Study** (Blog post format)
  - Structure (1,500-2,000 words):
    - **Headline**: "[Client Industry] Company Saves [X Hours] Weekly with Sales Automation"
    - **Introduction**: Client background (2 paragraphs)
    - **The Challenge**: Detailed problem description (3 paragraphs)
    - **The Solution**: What you implemented (4 paragraphs)
      - Week-by-week breakdown
      - Technical details (tools used, architecture)
      - Challenges overcome
    - **The Results**: Specific outcomes (3 paragraphs)
      - Time saved: X hours/week
      - Revenue impact: $X added to pipeline
      - Efficiency gains: X% improvement
      - Team satisfaction: Quotes from interviews
    - **Key Takeaways**: 3-5 lessons learned
    - **Call-to-Action**: "Want similar results?"
  - Include 5-10 images:
    - Client logo (with permission)
    - Before/after screenshots
    - Workflow diagrams
    - Results charts/graphs
  - Publish to website blog

- [ ] **Create Short-Form Versions**
  - **One-Page PDF** (designed in Canva):
    - Problem → Solution → Results (with big numbers)
    - Client quote + photo
    - Your logo and CTA
  - **LinkedIn Carousel** (10 slides):
    - Slide 1: Client logo + headline
    - Slides 2-3: The problem
    - Slides 4-7: The solution
    - Slides 8-9: The results (big numbers)
    - Slide 10: CTA
  - **Twitter/X Thread** (8-12 tweets):
    - Hook tweet with results
    - Thread breaking down story
    - End with case study link

- [ ] **Get Client Permission**
  - Send case study draft for approval
  - Request permission to use:
    - Company name and logo
    - Specific metrics
    - Video testimonial
  - Get signed release form
  - Make any requested changes

#### Target: Complete 2-3 Case Studies This Month

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Consistency > Intensity**
   - 2 hours daily LinkedIn outreach beats 20-hour weekly sprints
   - Never stop marketing, even when busy

2. **Niche Down**
   - Stay focused on professional services firms
   - Become the expert in ONE vertical

3. **Value-Based Pricing**
   - Frame against cost of current manual processes
   - Present proposals live, not via email

4. **Retainers = Business Stability**
   - Start conversations by Month 3
   - Target 40-50% conversion by Month 6

5. **Document Everything**
   - Build systems while you're small
   - Case studies are your #1 sales asset

6. **Don't Hire Too Early**
   - Wait until consistent $35-40K monthly for 3+ months
   - Hire for specific skill gaps, not general help

7. **Your Technical Background = Competitive Advantage**
   - Show the "how" that generic agencies can't
   - Use it in positioning and content
