# Discovery Conversation Guide

A structured approach to exploring pain points and validating AI sales agent use cases with potential pilot participants.

> **Goal:** Understand their business deeply before proposing solutions. Listen more than you talk. The best pilots come from real problems, not assumed ones.

---

## Before the Conversation

### Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Understand their business basics (what they do, who they serve)
- [ ] Look at their website/Google listing - how do leads currently find them?
- [ ] Note any obvious gaps (no contact form, phone only, slow response on Google reviews)
- [ ] Have specific questions ready, not generic ones

### Mindset

You're not selling. You're learning:
- What problems actually exist?
- How painful are those problems?
- Would an AI agent actually solve them?
- Is this a good pilot candidate?

---

## Conversation Structure

### Part 1: Understanding Their Business (10-15 min)

Start broad, then narrow in on the sales/lead handling process.

**Opening:**
> "I'm exploring some AI tools that might help service businesses like yours. Before I show you anything, I want to understand how your business actually works. Can you walk me through a typical week?"

**Business Understanding Questions:**

1. **"How do most of your customers find you?"**
   - Google search? Referrals? Repeat customers?
   - *Listen for:* Multiple channels, inconsistent tracking

2. **"When someone wants to hire you, what's the process from first contact to booked job?"**
   - Phone call? Form? Email? Walk through step by step
   - *Listen for:* Manual steps, delays, handoffs

3. **"Who handles incoming inquiries? Is that their main job or one of many?"**
   - *Listen for:* Owner doing everything, part-time admin, after-hours gaps

4. **"What happens if someone calls/emails outside business hours?"**
   - Voicemail? Nothing? Missed opportunity?
   - *Listen for:* Lost leads, delayed responses

---

### Part 2: Exploring Pain Points (15-20 min)

Dig into specific problems. Use their language, not yours.

**Lead Handling Questions:**

5. **"How quickly do you typically respond to a new inquiry?"**
   - Minutes? Hours? Next day?
   - *Listen for:* Honest delays, awareness of the problem

6. **"What happens when you're busy on a job and leads come in?"**
   - *Listen for:* Missed calls, delayed quotes, lost jobs

7. **"Have you ever lost a job because you took too long to respond?"**
   - Get specific examples if they have them
   - *Listen for:* Actual revenue impact, emotional frustration

8. **"What percentage of inquiries turn into actual jobs?"**
   - If they don't know, that's a signal too
   - *Listen for:* Low conversion, no tracking

**Quoting/Admin Questions:**

9. **"Walk me through how you create a quote. How long does it take?"**
   - *Listen for:* Manual processes, time sinks, context switching

10. **"What information do you need to give a quote? Do customers usually provide it all upfront?"**
    - *Listen for:* Back-and-forth, missing info, delays

11. **"How do you follow up with people who got a quote but haven't responded?"**
    - *Listen for:* No system, inconsistent follow-up, lost opportunities

12. **"What's the most annoying admin task you do repeatedly?"**
    - *Listen for:* Patterns, automatable tasks, emotional response

---

### Part 3: Quantifying the Problem (5-10 min)

Try to put numbers on the pain. This helps prioritize and later measure impact.

**Quantification Questions:**

13. **"Roughly how many inquiries do you get per week/month?"**
    - Helps understand volume

14. **"What's your average job value?"**
    - Helps calculate impact of missed leads

15. **"If you had to guess, how many leads slip through the cracks each month?"**
    - 1-2? 5-10? They often underestimate

16. **"How many hours per week do you spend on quoting and follow-up?"**
    - Helps quantify time savings potential

**Quick Math (do this in your head, don't say it out loud):**
- Missed leads/month × Average job value = Revenue leak
- Hours on admin × Their hourly rate = Time cost

---

### Part 4: Exploring Fit for AI Agent (10 min)

Now you can start connecting their problems to potential solutions.

**Fit Assessment Questions:**

17. **"If someone could respond to every inquiry within 5 minutes, 24/7, what would that be worth to you?"**
    - *Listen for:* Interest level, skepticism, specific scenarios

18. **"What if a system could ask qualifying questions (budget, timeline, job details) before you even talk to them?"**
    - *Listen for:* Which questions matter most to them

19. **"Would it help if quotes for standard jobs were generated automatically for you to review?"**
    - *Listen for:* Is quoting actually a bottleneck? What's "standard"?

20. **"What would you need to see to believe this could work for your business?"**
    - *Listen for:* Proof points they need, concerns to address

---

### Part 5: Pilot Fit Assessment (5 min)

Determine if they're a good pilot candidate.

**Pilot Suitability Questions:**

21. **"If we built something that addressed [specific pain point they mentioned], would you be open to testing it?"**

22. **"We're looking for pilot partners who can share their experience publicly - case study, testimonial. Would you be comfortable with that?"**
    - This is your non-negotiable

23. **"What would make this a success for you in 90 days?"**
    - Helps define pilot success criteria

## Red Flags (Not a Good Pilot Fit)

- **No clear pain:** They're "doing fine" - don't force it
- **Unrealistic expectations:** Want AI to replace all human interaction
- **No willingness to share publicly:** Violates your non-negotiable
- **Too small volume:** <10 leads/month may not show meaningful results
- **Resistant to change:** "We've always done it this way"
- **Looking for free work:** Only interested because it's free, no intent to pay later

---

## Green Flags (Good Pilot Fit)

- **Specific, quantifiable pain:** "I lose 2-3 jobs a month because I can't respond fast enough"
- **Motivated to solve it:** They've tried other solutions or thought about hiring
- **Reasonable volume:** Enough leads to demonstrate impact
- **Willing to engage:** Will give feedback, participate in case study
- **Budget potential:** Even if pilot is free, they could afford to pay later
- **Good communicator:** Easy to work with, responsive

---

## Use Case Signals to Listen For

### Strong AI Sales Agent Fit:
- High inquiry volume, low response capacity
- Repetitive qualifying questions (budget, timeline, location, job type)
- Standard services with predictable pricing
- After-hours lead loss
- Quote generation is time-consuming but formulaic

### Weaker Fit (still possible, but harder):
- Every job is completely custom
- Pricing requires site visit
- Very low volume (<10/month)
- Highly relationship-driven sales
- Complex technical sales requiring expertise

## Sample Conversation Flow (Locksmith Example)

> **You:** "When someone needs a locksmith, how do they usually find you?"
>
> **Them:** "Mostly Google. Some word of mouth."
>
> **You:** "And when they find you on Google, what do they do - call or fill out a form?"
>
> **Them:** "Usually call. I get a lot of calls."
>
> **You:** "What happens when you're in the middle of a job and the phone rings?"
>
> **Them:** "It goes to voicemail. I try to call back when I'm done but sometimes it's hours later."
>
> **You:** "Do you think you lose jobs because of that?"
>
> **Them:** "Definitely. Especially emergencies - they just call the next guy."
>
> **You:** "How many calls would you guess you miss in a typical week?"
>
> **Them:** "Maybe 5-10? Hard to say."
>
> **You:** "And what's a typical job worth?"
>
> **Them:** "$150-300 for a lockout, more for rekeying or commercial."
>
> **You:** "So potentially $500-2000 a week in missed calls..."
>
> *[Now you've quantified the problem and can explore solutions]*
