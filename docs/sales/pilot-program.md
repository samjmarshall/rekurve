# Rekurve AI Release Pilot Program

> **Purpose:** Validate AI sales agent use cases with real businesses before general availability. Build case studies and testimonials to support future paid offerings.

---

## Program Overview

### What is the Release Pilot?

The Release Pilot is a free, limited program where we build and deploy AI sales agents for qualified businesses. In exchange for free implementation, participants agree to:

1. Provide ongoing feedback during the pilot
2. Participate in a public case study
3. Provide a testimonial if results are positive

### Why We're Doing This

We're pre-product-market-fit. We need to:
- Validate that AI sales agents solve real problems
- Learn which use cases work best
- Build proof points (case studies, testimonials) for future sales
- Refine our implementation process

### Program Stages

| Stage | Name | Status | Description |
|-------|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Release Pilot** | Now Open | Foundation-tier features, limited spots |
| 2 | **Beta** | Coming Soon | Growth-tier features, for successful pilot graduates |
| 3 | **General Availability** | Future | Paid offerings for new customers |

## Qualification Criteria

### Non-Negotiable Requirements

These are must-haves. No exceptions.

| Requirement | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Case study commitment** | We need public proof points to sell to future customers |
| **Testimonial commitment** | Social proof is essential for credibility |
| **Feedback participation** | We're learning - we need honest input on what works and what doesn't |
| **Decision-maker involvement** | Need someone who can approve changes and provide timely feedback |

### Ideal Candidate Profile

Prioritize applicants who match these criteria:

**Business Characteristics:**
- [ ] Service-based business (trades, professional services, agencies)
- [ ] Receives inbound leads regularly (10+ per month minimum)
- [ ] Has a defined service/product with somewhat predictable pricing
- [ ] Located in Australia (Brisbane/Melbourne preferred for in-person if needed)

**Pain Point Signals:**
- [ ] Slow response to leads (hours or days, not minutes)
- [ ] Missing leads due to capacity constraints
- [ ] Manual quoting process that takes significant time
- [ ] No after-hours lead capture
- [ ] Owner/team spending significant time on admin vs. billable work

**Engagement Signals:**
- [ ] Enthusiastic about trying AI (not just "free stuff")
- [ ] Responsive during discovery conversations
- [ ] Willing to invest time in feedback and iteration
- [ ] Has realistic expectations (AI augments, doesn't replace humans)

**Future Potential:**
- [ ] Could afford paid offering later ($1,500+/month)
- [ ] Business is growing or wants to grow
- [ ] Would be a good reference customer for their industry

### Red Flags (Decline or Deprioritize)

- Only interested because it's free, no intent to pay later
- Unrealistic expectations ("AI will replace my whole sales team")
- Unresponsive during discovery phase
- Very low lead volume (<10/month) - hard to demonstrate impact
- Business model unclear or unstable
- Resistant to change or technology adoption
- Unwilling to commit to case study/testimonial

## What Pilot Participants Receive

### Foundation Tier (Release Pilot)

**Implementation:**
- Single channel AI agent (email OR SMS OR web chat)
- Basic CRM setup and integration
- Lead qualification logic tailored to their business
- Automated response sequences (3-5 sequences)
- Basic lead scoring
- Performance dashboard

**Support:**
- 30-day optimization support
- Email support during pilot
- Bi-weekly check-in calls (15-30 min)

**Timeline:**
- Week 1-2: Discovery and setup
- Week 3-4: Build and test
- Week 5-8: Live pilot with optimization
- Week 9-12: Evaluate results and document

### Growth Tier (Beta - Future)

For successful Release Pilot participants only:
- Multi-channel integration
- AI-powered qualification
- Advanced analytics
- 90-day optimization
- Priority support

## Pilot Success Metrics

We'll measure success across these dimensions:

### Quantitative Metrics

| Metric | How We Measure | Target |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| Response time | Time from lead inquiry to first AI response | <5 minutes |
| Lead capture rate | Leads captured vs. total inquiries | >90% |
| Qualification accuracy | Correctly qualified leads / total qualified | >80% |
| Time saved | Hours of manual work replaced per week | 5+ hours |
| Leads processed | Total leads handled by AI per month | Depends on volume |

### Qualitative Metrics

- Participant satisfaction with the solution
- Quality of AI responses (reviewed together)
- Ease of integration with existing workflow
- Learnings for product improvement

### Case Study Metrics

For the public case study, we'll want to document:
- Before state (response times, manual hours, pain points)
- After state (improved metrics)
- Specific results (leads captured, time saved, etc.)
- Participant quote/testimonial

## Pilot Timeline

### Standard 12-Week Pilot

```
Week 1-2: Discovery & Audit
├── Discovery conversation (pain points, current process)
├── Access to systems (CRM, email, etc.)
├── Document current state metrics
└── Define success criteria

Week 3-4: Build & Configure
├── Build AI agent tailored to their business
├── Configure qualification logic
├── Set up integrations
└── Internal testing

Week 5-8: Live Pilot
├── Launch AI agent on live leads
├── Monitor and optimize daily
├── Bi-weekly check-in calls
└── Iterate based on feedback

Week 9-12: Evaluate & Document
├── Measure results against success criteria
├── Document case study
├── Collect testimonial
├── Discuss next steps (Beta or end)
```

## Application Process

### Step 1: Initial Application
- Applicant fills out form on website
- Basic information: company, role, industry, lead volume, pain points

### Step 2: Discovery Call (15-30 min)
- Use Discovery Conversation Guide
- Assess fit against qualification criteria
- Explain pilot expectations clearly

### Step 3: Decision
- Accept, waitlist, or decline within 48 hours
- If accepted, send Pilot Agreement

### Step 4: Pilot Agreement
- Participant signs simple agreement confirming:
  - Case study commitment
  - Testimonial commitment
  - Feedback participation
  - Access provision

### Step 5: Kickoff
- Schedule kickoff call
- Begin Week 1 activities

## Managing the Pilot Pipeline

### Tracking Applicants

Keep a simple spreadsheet or CRM with:
- Company name
- Contact name/email
- Industry
- Lead volume (self-reported)
- Discovery call date
- Qualification score (1-5)
- Status (Applied, Discovery, Accepted, Active, Completed, Declined)
- Notes

### Capacity Planning

For the initial Release Pilot:
- **Target:** 2-3 active pilots at a time
- **Rationale:** Enough to learn patterns, not so many you can't give attention
- **Waitlist:** If more qualified applicants, add to waitlist for next cohort

### Saying No Gracefully

For applicants who don't qualify:

> "Thanks for your interest in the Release Pilot. After reviewing your application, we don't think we're the right fit for your business right now.
>
> [Optional: specific reason - e.g., "Your lead volume is lower than what we need to demonstrate meaningful results."]
>
> We'd be happy to keep you on our list for future programs. In the meantime, [suggest alternative if appropriate]."

## Learning Capture

After each pilot, document:

1. **What worked well?**
2. **What didn't work?**
3. **What would we do differently?**
4. **What features/capabilities are missing?**
5. **Is this industry/use case a good fit?**
6. **Would this participant be a good reference?**

Use these learnings to:
- Refine qualification criteria
- Improve the implementation process
- Identify ideal customer profile
- Build product roadmap
